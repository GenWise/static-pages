How It Works

Format, schedule, entry points, and fees—everything you need to know.

Programme Format

Live Sessions

  • Science: 2 hours per week (Friday evenings). Integrated Physics, Chemistry, Biology.
  • Mathematics: 1.5 hours per week (Saturdays).
  • Students can take Science only, Mathematics only, or both.

How Classes Work

  • Live online sessions via Zoom with expert mentors
  • Small cohorts: 20–30 students per group
  • Discussion-based: Students think, argue, explain—not passive listening
  • Teaching assistant support for deeper exploration and homework help

Independent Work

Expect 2–3 hours per week: readings, short investigations, problem sets. Less time than most competitive activities. Enough to stretch the mind, never to burn it out.

Recordings

Session recordings available for 3 days when students must miss class. However, live participation is strongly encouraged—the discussion and peer interaction are central to learning.

Technology Requirement

Students should join sessions from a laptop or computer, not a phone or tablet. A stable internet connection and a quiet space for focused participation are essential.

Schedule & Entry Points

Programme Duration

30 weeks across the academic year, organised into six 5-week modules.

Entry Points

There are six entry points across the year—at the start of each module. If you find us mid-year, you haven't missed the opportunity. We can place your child into a suitable cohort at the next entry point.

Trial Period

New students begin with a 1-month trial (one full module). This lets families experience TNP365 before committing to the full year.

  • Pay ₹20,000 booking fee at the start of the trial
  • If you continue, the trial fee adjusts against the annual fee
  • If it's not the right fit, the booking fee is fully refundable
  • No pressure to continue

During the trial, students can explore both subjects before deciding which to continue with.

A Multi-Year Journey

TNP365 is designed as a 2–3 year pathway, not a one-off course.

Many students join in Grade 7 or 8 and continue with the same cohort over multiple years. As the cohort progresses, we design new modules and challenges at their level—instead of making them repeat the same content.

Over time, students build a portfolio of explanations, investigations, and projects that shows what they can really do—not just the marks they scored. They develop roughly Grade 10+ breadth of ideas with much greater depth, and the habits of rigorous, joyful, self-directed learning.
